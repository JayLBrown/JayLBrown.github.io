My aspiration to become a data scientist stems preeminently from my passion for data ethics, AI, and policy reform across multiple domains. While the design, development, and implementation of initiatives for justice have always been data-driven, at no point prior have the tools to narrate the stories of underrepresented populations of people been so accessible. I believe these stories are critical to structural change at every level. So, it is for these reasons that I commit myself to a life of advocacy through technological skills.

Last summer, I worked as a researcher at the University of California, where I explored misinformation and disenfranchisement operations on social media platforms, with a deep focus on understanding how misinformation influences public opinion.

In 2019, I worked under Dr. Sweeney at Harvard's Data Privacy Lab on multiple projects that assessed the strength of the HIPPA Privacy Rule and measured voter susceptibility to fraud. For each project, I developed strategies that leveraged multiple Python scientific libraries and applied descriptive and inferential statistical methods where required.
